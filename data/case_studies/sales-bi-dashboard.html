This case study describes a sales performance dashboard built as a decision interface: consistent KPI definitions, a clean dimensional model, and drill-through views that let leaders move from “what happened” to “where and why” without spreadsheet ping-pong.

Executive Summary

  • Single source of truth: revenue and margin KPIs defined once (DAX), reused everywhere.
  • Time intelligence: YoY/MoM trends, variance, and contribution analysis for leadership reviews.
  • Drill-through: from executive KPIs → region/segment/SKU in a few clicks.
  • Actionability: makes margin erosion and underperforming SKUs visible early.

Problem

Leaders need fast answers: “Are we growing?”, “Where is margin leaking?”, “Which segments need attention?”. When data lives across spreadsheets and inconsistent definitions, teams argue about numbers instead of decisions.

Model & KPI Design

  • Star-ish schema: sales fact at transaction/day grain + dimensions (date, region, segment, product/SKU).
  • Core measures: revenue, cost, gross margin, margin %, growth rates, mix contributions.
  • Guardrails: explicit filters for returns/discounts so margin math stays honest.

Dashboard UX

  1. Executive header. KPI cards + trend spark-lines for fast scanning.
  2. Where to look. Region/segment breakdowns surface concentration and weak spots.
  3. Why it changed. Mix/variance visuals show whether growth is volume-driven or price-driven.
  4. Drill-through. Region → SKU/customer so teams can act without exporting data.

Findings (example patterns)

  • Margin erosion: two regions showing growing revenue but falling margin %—often discount or product-mix driven.
  • SKU outliers: a small set of SKUs account for a disproportionate share of margin leakage.
  • Mix shift: growth coming from lower-margin segments, requiring pricing or bundle adjustments.

Next Steps

  • Alerting: threshold alerts for margin % drops and unusual discount spikes.
  • Ownership: KPI owners + weekly review cadence; decisions logged against KPI movements.
  • Data contracts: schema tests for cost/discount columns so calculations don’t silently break.
