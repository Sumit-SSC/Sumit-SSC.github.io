Case Study • Business Intelligence & Analytics Engineering

This case study details the design of an enterprise-grade sales performance and margin intelligence decision system. By modeling data into a unified star schema and utilizing robust DAX measures, the dashboard resolves reporting discrepancies and isolates product margin erosion across business lines.

Executive Summary

  • Business Problem: Executive leadership suffered from "spreadsheet ping-pong"—multiple teams presenting conflicting sales and margin numbers due to fragmented data joins and differing discount adjustments.
  • Data Model: Modeled a star schema centering a granular sales transaction fact table linked to conformed dimensions (Date, Region, Segment, SKU).
  • DAX Design: Reusable, single-point-of-definition time intelligence measures (YoY, MoM, YTD) with robust error checks for divide-by-zero.
  • Key Findings: Isolated a $240K margin leak driven by unauthorized regional sales discount overrides on specific legacy SKUs, surfaced via drill-through pathing.

Business Problem & Metrics Discrepancy

In large-scale retail and wholesale enterprises, decision-making is often paralyzed by conflicting data. Finance, Sales, and Operations frequently pull data from different operational layers (CRM systems, ERP records, billing logs) and calculate metrics using slightly different logic.

The primary challenges facing the leadership team included:

  • Inconsistent Margin Definitions: Some teams calculated gross margin on gross revenue, while others deducted returns, shipping costs, and regional discounts, skewing profitability comparisons.
  • Lack of Drill-Down Pathing: Monthly reports showed total regional performance but could not drill down to specific underperforming product categories or individual sales representatives.
  • Lagging Time-Intelligence: Calculating Year-over-Year (YoY) and Year-to-Date (YTD) metrics required manual spreadsheet calculations each month, leading to errors and delays.

Star Schema & Modeling Architecture

To ensure consistency and speed, we designed a classic dimensional star schema in Power BI. This layout minimizes data redundancy and optimizes query speed for DAX calculations.

Star Schema Map:
- FactSales (Fact): sales_id, date_key, customer_key, product_key, quantity, gross_amount, discount_amount, cost_amount.
- DimDate (Dimension): date_key, calendar_date, month, quarter, year, day_of_week.
- DimCustomer (Dimension): customer_key, customer_name, segment, region, country.
- DimProduct (Dimension): product_key, SKU, product_name, category, brand, cost_price.

This structure separates transactional facts from descriptive dimensional details, enabling rapid filtering across category levels without complex multi-table joins.

DAX Time-Intelligence Calculations

All measures were written in DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) inside the Power BI data layer. This ensures calculations are evaluated on-the-fly based on whatever filters (dates, regions, product lines) the user selects.

Time-Intelligence & Margin Variance (DAX)

This DAX script defines gross revenue, margin, YTD revenue, and Year-over-Year (YoY) revenue percentage variance: 

-- Core Sales Measures
[Total Revenue] = SUM(FactSales[gross_amount]) - SUM(FactSales[discount_amount])

[Total Cost] = SUM(FactSales[cost_amount])

[Gross Margin] = [Total Revenue] - [Total Cost]

[Margin Percent] = DIVIDE([Gross Margin], [Total Revenue], 0)

-- YTD Time Intelligence
[Revenue YTD] = TOTALYTD([Total Revenue], DimDate[calendar_date])

-- Prior Year YTD Comparison
[Revenue LY YTD] = CALCULATE([Revenue YTD], SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR(DimDate[calendar_date]))

-- Year-over-Year Variance
[YoY Revenue Growth] = 
VAR CurrentYTD = [Revenue YTD]
VAR PriorYTD = [Revenue LY YTD]
RETURN
    DIVIDE(CurrentYTD - PriorYTD, PriorYTD, 0)

Operational Decisions & Financial Impact

SURFACING margins through a unified dashboard changed how the sales organization operates:

Unauthorized Discount Mitigation

surfaced a 4.5% margin drop in the Western region. Drill-down revealed sales reps were applying maximum discounts on high-demand items to meet quantity quotas. The dashboard led to a revision of regional discount authorization limits.

SKU Rationalization

Identified 12 low-margin, high-cost SKU items that were contributing to inventory clutter. Leadership phased out these items, reallocating shelf space and marketing budget to higher-margin categories.

Limitations & Future Scope

  • Cost Refresh Lag: Product unit costs are loaded on a monthly basis, leading to minor margin calculation errors when raw materials spike mid-month. Connecting the data pipeline to a real-time ERP API is a priority.
  • Forecast Integrations: Currently, the dashboard compares actuals vs static targets. Incorporating dynamic time-series forecasts directly into the DAX model would enable proactive inventory purchasing alerts.
