This case study documents an end-to-end regression workflow to estimate fair-market used-car prices from listing attributes. The goal is not “build a model”, but deliver a pricing decision tool that is accurate, explainable, and usable by non-technical teams.

Executive Summary

  • Outcome: Tuned gradient-boosted trees achieve RMSE under $3,000 with stable generalization on a held-out split.
  • Top drivers: Mileage, vehicle age, and brand segment explain most of the price variance; fuel and drivetrain add smaller lift.
  • Explainability: SHAP summaries translate model behavior into stakeholder-friendly drivers for pricing conversations.
  • Operationalization: A Streamlit app provides real-time estimates + uncertainty guidance; PBIX/slides enable leadership reviews.

Problem

Used-car pricing is high leverage: mispricing creates inventory holding cost (overpriced) or margin leakage (underpriced). Manual rules like “mileage-only” miss important interactions (brand × mileage, age × drivetrain) and are inconsistent across sales teams.

Data & Assumptions

  • Input: Listing-level structured attributes (year, mileage, brand, fuel, drivetrain, etc.).
  • Target: Sale/list price (modeled as a continuous value).
  • Constraints: Real deployments need geo/seasonality; this version focuses on attribute-driven pricing and explainability.

Methodology

  1. Profiling & cleaning. Normalize categories (e.g., Petrol/Gasoline), handle missing optional fields, and remove obvious entry errors/outliers.
  2. Feature engineering. Derive vehicle age, mileage bins, brand segment, and interaction features (age × mileage) that capture non-linear effects.
  3. Modeling. Benchmark tree-based models (Random Forest / XGBoost / LightGBM) with cross-validation and a time-aware holdout split where possible.
  4. Explainability. Use SHAP to show global drivers (summary) and per-listing reasoning (why this car is above/below a reference baseline).

Results

  • Performance: XGBoost baseline + tuning reaches ~$2.8k RMSE with strong \(R^2\) (reported ~85%).
  • Robustness: Tuning reduces overfit; holdout performance stays close to train error.
  • Interpretability: Driver ordering aligns with market intuition while still capturing complex interactions.

Decision Enablement

The “product” here is a pricing workflow: a quick estimate + rationale. The Streamlit UI supports front-line usage; the PBIX/slides support internal reviews and stakeholder alignment.

Limitations & Next Steps

  • Geo & seasonality: Add region features and seasonal trends for better market realism.
  • External signals: Fuel price, macro conditions, and inventory levels often shift price curves materially.
  • Uncertainty: Calibrate prediction intervals and add “confidence bands” to guide negotiation risk.
