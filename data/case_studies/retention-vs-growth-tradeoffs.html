Why It Matters: Balance acquisition and retention spend.

Growth teams face a fundamental trade-off: acquisition (new users) vs. retention (keep existing users). This case study examines how to balance acquisition and retention spend using cohort economics and payback periods—using Reforge and HBR growth trade-offs.

Executive Summary

  • Cohort economics: LTV and CAC by acquisition cohort (channel, time); compare payback period and LTV/CAC across cohorts to prioritize where to invest.
  • Payback periods: Time for cumulative revenue (or margin) to equal CAC; shorter payback = faster capital recovery and lower risk. Use to compare acquisition channels and retention quality.
  • Retention vs acquisition spend: Improving retention (e.g., product, engagement) extends LTV and effective payback; improving acquisition (e.g., paid) increases volume but at higher CAC. Optimal mix depends on LTV/CAC and payback by channel.

Business Context & Problem Framing

Reforge's growth loops and HBR's "right way to grow" describe how sustainable growth requires balancing acquisition and retention. Over-investing in acquisition with weak retention burns capital; over-investing in retention with no acquisition caps growth. Cohort economics (LTV, CAC, payback by cohort) informs where to spend.

Data Landscape & Event Model

Cohorts: sign-up date, channel, segment. For each cohort: CAC (acquisition cost / new users), cumulative revenue (or margin) by month, LTV (modeled or observed), payback month (when cumulative revenue ≥ CAC). Retention: retention curve by cohort. Compare cohorts to rank channels and inform spend.

Metrics, KPIs & North Star

Primary: LTV/CAC and payback period by cohort/channel. Secondary: retention curve, CAC trend. North star: sustainable growth—LTV/CAC above threshold and payback within target, with balanced acquisition and retention spend.

Analytical / Experimental Approach

Build cohort tables (CAC, LTV, payback by cohort and channel); rank channels by LTV/CAC and payback; identify underperforming channels. A/B test acquisition spend (e.g., increase spend on best channel) vs retention (e.g., product or engagement experiment); measure incremental LTV and payback. Use payback and LTV/CAC to set acquisition and retention budget mix.

AI & Automation Layer

Automated cohort and LTV/CAC reporting; payback and LTV/CAC by channel; optional LTV prediction by cohort; alerts when payback or LTV/CAC degrades; LLM summarization of "where to invest" for strategy.

Observations & Key Findings

Cohort economics reveals which channels are profitable and how fast they pay back; retention improvements extend LTV and improve payback without increasing CAC; optimal mix shifts as channels and product mature.

Trade-offs, Risks & Failure Modes

Trade-off: acquisition volume vs retention quality. Risk: LTV model misspecification; attribution bias. Failure mode: optimizing only acquisition or only retention without cohort view.

Business Impact & Decision Outcomes

Decision: use cohort economics (LTV, CAC, payback) to balance acquisition and retention spend; prioritize channels and retention initiatives by payback and LTV/CAC. Outcome: sustainable growth with capital efficiency.

Limitations & What We'd Improve

LTV and attribution are uncertain; long payback cohorts need long observation. Improvements: causal attribution; probabilistic LTV; integration with marketing and product experiments.

Future Scope (12–24 Months)

Real-time LTV/CAC and payback; automated budget allocation; cross-channel attribution.

Key Takeaway

Balance acquisition and retention using cohort economics: LTV, CAC, and payback by cohort and channel. Use payback and LTV/CAC to set acquisition and retention spend so growth is sustainable and capital-efficient.
