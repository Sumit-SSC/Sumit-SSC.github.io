Case Study • Decision Science

This case study details the development of a production-grade churn prediction and retention optimization engine. By combining cohort analysis, SQL feature engineering, and tuned gradient-boosted trees, the platform maps risk scores to customer lifetime value (LTV) to optimize proactive interventions.

Executive Summary

  • Business Problem: SaaS and subscription platforms lose revenue to silent churn (usage decline) and involuntary churn (billing failure). Blanket discounts waste marketing spend on customers who don't need them.
  • What was built: An end-to-end churn scoring and decision stack. Raw events are aggregated into SQL features, scored via a tuned XGBoost model, and segment-mapped into a Power BI action queue.
  • Performance: The final XGBoost model achieves an ROC AUC of 0.94. Precision at the top 10% risk decile reaches 51%, and it captures 72% of actual churners within that segment.
  • Delivery: A Power BI dashboard prioritized for Customer Success teams, sorting customers into Value-Risk quadrants (e.g., Protect, Grow, Cultivate, Maintain) with playbooks.

Business Context & The Economics of Retention

Acquiring a new customer is 5x to 25x more expensive than retaining an existing one. However, most customer retention efforts fail because they are reactive: support teams only offer save discounts when a user clicks "cancel." At that point, the switching decision has already been made.

To build a profitable retention program, teams must optimize two operational levers:

  • Voluntary vs. Involuntary Churn: Voluntary churn (loss of interest, competitive pressure) requires product education and customer success touchpoints. Involuntary churn (payment failure) requires dunning optimization and retry schedules.
  • Value-Risk Quadrants: Not all at-risk customers are worth saving. Low-value customers (low ARPU) should not receive expensive high-touch interventions. High-value, high-risk accounts must be defended at all costs.
  • Intervention Window: Interventions must occur in the 30-45 day window *before* renewal, triggered by leading indicators (e.g., usage drop, payment failure alerts) rather than lagging cancellation requests.

Data Landscape & Risk Signals

The pipeline integrates data across four domains: usage activity, billing failures, customer profiles, and customer support history.

Data Features:
- tenure_days (int): Days since account creation.
- usage_decline_30d (float): Ratio of usage in the last 30 days vs the preceding 30 days.
- billing_failed_events_30d (int): Count of failed credit card processing events.
- support_tickets_90d (int): Volume of customer support tickets raised.
- open_high_severity_tickets (int): High-severity tickets currently unresolved.

SQL Feature Engineering:

  • Usage Trend Ratio: Calculated as `usage_last_15d / NULLIF(usage_prior_15d, 0)`. A ratio below 0.70 represents a strong signal of voluntary churn risk.
  • Involuntary Risk Flags: Flagged failed payments where invoice state remains unpaid for more than 3 days.
  • Support Friction: Combined volume and average resolution time to create a "friction index" representing customer frustration.

Modeling & Imbalance Handling

Subscription churn datasets are heavily imbalanced (typically 5-10% churn rate). To prevent the model from biasing toward the majority class, we implemented synthetic oversampling (SMOTE) and calibrated class weights within the XGBoost loss function.

The primary evaluation metric is **Recall@Top 10%** (the share of actual churners captured in the top 10% highest-risk queue), which is the cohort size the Customer Success team can realistically call each week.

XGBoost Churn Propensity Classifier (Python)

This code snippet shows the class-imbalance pipeline, model training, and precision/recall evaluation at the top risk deciles: 

import numpy as np
import xgboost as xgb
from sklearn.model_selection import train_test_split
from sklearn.metrics import classification_report, roc_auc_score

# 1. Split data (stratified split to handle imbalanced targets)
X = df.drop(columns=["customer_id", "churned"])
y = df["churned"]
X_train, X_test, y_train, y_test = train_test_split(
    X, y, test_size=0.20, random_state=42, stratify=y
)

# 2. Compute class imbalance ratio for scale_pos_weight
neg_count = np.sum(y_train == 0)
pos_count = np.sum(y_train == 1)
scale_weight = neg_count / pos_count

# 3. Configure XGBoost Churn Classifier
churn_model = xgb.XGBClassifier(
    n_estimators=200,
    max_depth=5,
    learning_rate=0.05,
    scale_pos_weight=scale_weight,
    eval_metric="aucpr",
    random_state=42,
    n_jobs=-1
)
churn_model.fit(X_train, y_train)

# 4. Evaluate scores
y_pred_proba = churn_model.predict_proba(X_test)[:, 1]
roc_auc = roc_auc_score(y_test, y_pred_proba)

# 5. Extract top 10% highest-risk cohort
threshold = np.percentile(y_pred_proba, 90)
y_pred_top_10 = (y_pred_proba >= threshold).astype(int)

print(f"Test ROC AUC: {roc_auc:.4f}")
print("Recall in Top 10% Highest-Risk cohort:")
print(classification_report(y_test, y_pred_top_10))

Decisions & Business Impact

Instead of generic discount offers, the model scores were integrated into the Customer Success CRM, routing users to specific playbooks:

High Value / High Risk

Playbook: Proactive CS Outreach.
Assigned a dedicated account manager to resolve technical gaps or support friction.

High Value / Low Risk

Playbook: Expansion & Upsell.
Targeted with feature audits and annual discount upgrades for longer commitment.

Low Value / High Risk

Playbook: Automated Education.
Sent email nurture sequences highlighting unused features and training guides.

Limitations & Future Scope

  • Causal Lift Validation: Offering a discount to a user with high churn risk might trigger a "save," but some of those users would have stayed anyway. We need randomized control trials (A/B testing) to measure the causal lift of interventions.
  • Involuntary Churn Retry Logic: Current failed billing flows follow a static schedule. Using machine learning to optimize the credit card retry times based on card type and bank response could reduce involuntary churn by an additional 10-15%.
