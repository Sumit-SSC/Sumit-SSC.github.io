This project turns churn analytics into a retention operating workflow: define cohorts, engineer interpretable features, produce churn risk scores, and publish the result as a Power BI decision interface for retention teams.

Executive Summary

  • Outcome: churn propensity model + drivers surfaced as segments that teams can act on (not a “model demo”).
  • Key drivers: support-ticket volume/recency and payment failures are consistently high-signal churn predictors; usage decline follows.
  • Targeting logic: combine risk × value to prioritize high-value, high-risk accounts where interventions have best ROI.
  • Delivery: Power BI dashboard provides cohort trends, risk distributions, and driver summaries for playbooks.

Problem

Blanket discounts are expensive and often mis-targeted. The practical retention question is: Which customers are both likely to churn and valuable enough to justify an intervention? To answer that, we need risk scoring, explainability, and segmentation—then a dashboard that makes the result usable.

Data Model (conceptual)

  • Customer: signup date, tenure, plan tier, ARPU proxy.
  • Usage: activity counts, usage minutes, trend features (recent vs prior window).
  • Support: ticket volume, severity, recency, unresolved status.
  • Billing: payment failures, dunning flags, invoice delays.

Methodology

  1. Cohorting. Define cohorts by signup month and lifecycle stage so churn rates are comparable over time.
  2. Feature engineering. Build risk signals that are stable and explainable (trends, recency, counts).
  3. Modeling. Train an XGBoost classifier with imbalance handling; evaluate with AUC + precision at top risk deciles.
  4. Explainability. Use SHAP to rank drivers globally and for individual accounts.
  5. Segmentation. Map customers into value–risk quadrants; define interventions per quadrant (save offers, proactive support, education).

What the dashboard enables

  • “Where is churn rising?” Cohort trend views and lifecycle breakdown.
  • “Who should we contact this week?” High-value / high-risk queue.
  • “Why are they risky?” Driver panels: payment failures, support intensity, usage drop.
  • “What should we do?” Playbook mapping and monitoring after intervention.

Limitations & Next Steps

  • Causal lift: validate interventions with holdouts (avoid “treat the treated” bias).
  • Monitoring: drift checks on risk distribution + driver shifts.
  • Feedback loop: capture intervention outcomes to improve targeting over time.
