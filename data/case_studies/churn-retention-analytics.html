This case study is written like a Decision Science retention program: model risk is only useful if it connects to interventions, ROI, and a measurement plan. The project includes both prediction and a retention strategy simulator.

Executive summary
  • Dataset: 92,610 customers with realistic usage, billing, and support behavior.
  • Models: Logistic Regression + Random Forest with explainability.
  • Performance (project): RF ROC AUC 0.96; Precision@Top 10% = 51%.
  • Decisioning: retention simulator ties risk scores to ROI-based interventions.

1) Business context

Subscription businesses care about when churn can be influenced. If you detect risk too late, the customer is already gone; too early and you waste retention budget.

This project targets an intervention window (31–45 days) and focuses on making churn risk actionable for a retention team.

2) Data & feature engineering

The strongest churn signals usually combine usage trajectory + payment friction + support burden. The pipeline builds SQL feature tables that make those patterns explicit:

  • Usage trend: decline in recent period vs historical baseline
  • Billing: payment failures, invoice delays
  • Support: ticket volume, unresolved issues
  • Tenure & tier: lifecycle stage + plan-based risk differences

3) Modeling approach

  • Baseline: Logistic Regression for interpretability and calibration.
  • Non-linear lift: Random Forest to capture interactions (e.g., usage decline + payment issues).
  • Explainability: rank drivers for each customer/segment to guide interventions.

In a production setting, I’d add calibration (Platt/Isotonic), stability monitoring, and backtesting by cohort.

4) Results & business readout

  • Performance: RF ROC AUC 0.96; Precision@Top 10% = 51% (high-signal queue).
  • Top drivers: usage decline, payment issues, support load.
  • Segment risk: Free and Basic tiers higher churn vs Enterprise.
  • Impact framing: targeted interventions ~2.3x ROI for high-risk segments in the intervention window.

5) Strategy simulator (why it’s important)

Models often fail in orgs because the next question is: “Okay, what do we do now?” The retention simulator turns predictions into decisions:

  • Choose an offer strategy (discount, outreach, service credits)
  • Apply it to a risk segment
  • Estimate ROI and allocate budget where it performs best

6) Future work (advanced DS)

  • Uplift modeling: predict treatment effect (who will be saved by an offer), not just churn risk.
  • Causal evaluation: holdout groups + CUPED to improve measurement sensitivity.
  • Policy optimization: constrained optimization (budget, fairness, segment SLAs) for offer allocation.
  • Deployment: feature store + batch scoring + model monitoring + drift alerts.
