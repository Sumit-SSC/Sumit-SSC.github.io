Why It Matters: Dashboards that drive action.

BI dashboards should be decision interfaces—designed so that the right person sees the right metric at the right time and can act. This case study covers KPI layering, narrative dashboards, and exec vs ops views—using Gartner and Tableau dashboard design.

Executive Summary

  • KPI layering: Primary metric first; secondary and guardrails in context; avoid metric overload—one decision per view.
  • Narrative dashboards: Lead with the question or decision; use layout and order to tell a story (what happened, why, what to do).
  • Exec vs ops views: Exec: high-level, trend, exception; ops: granular, filterable, actionable. Same data, different slice and depth.

Business Context & Problem Framing

Gartner's BI best practices and Tableau's dashboard design guidance emphasize that dashboards fail when they show data without guiding the viewer to a decision. Design for the user (exec vs ops), the question (what happened vs what to do), and the action (monitor vs intervene).

Data Landscape & Event Model

Dashboards consume curated datasets (aggregated, filtered) with clear definitions. Semantic layer or metric definitions (e.g., "active user" = definition + dimensions) ensure consistency. Exec and ops views may use the same base tables with different aggregation and filters.

Metrics, KPIs & North Star

Primary KPI per dashboard or section; secondary in context; guardrails (e.g., data freshness) visible. North star: dashboard drives at least one clear decision per user type.

Analytical / Experimental Approach

User research: what decision does the viewer need? Map metrics to decisions; design layout (narrative order, hierarchy). A/B test layout or default filters (e.g., time range) to measure engagement and action (e.g., clicks, exports, follow-up).

AI & Automation Layer

Auto-generated narrative summaries (LLM) from dashboard data; anomaly highlights; optional natural-language questions that drive to the right dashboard or slice.

Observations & Key Findings

Dashboards that lead with a question and one primary metric get more action; exec and ops need different depth; narrative order and exception highlighting improve usefulness.

Trade-offs, Risks & Failure Modes

Trade-off: simplicity (few metrics) vs. completeness (many metrics). Risk: dashboard becomes a data dump. Failure mode: building for "everyone" and satisfying no one.

Business Impact & Decision Outcomes

Decision: design dashboards as decision interfaces—KPI layering, narrative, exec vs ops. Outcome: higher engagement and clearer action from BI.

Limitations & What We'd Improve

Static dashboards can lag behind questions; personalization is limited. Improvements: adaptive defaults, NL-to-dashboard, and tighter link to alerts and workflows.

Future Scope (12–24 Months)

Conversational BI; dashboards that suggest next question or action; integration with decision automation.
