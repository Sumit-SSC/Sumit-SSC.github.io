This case study shows a practical A/B testing readout that product and leadership can trust. The emphasis is a repeatable decision workflow: define metrics, confirm randomization, estimate effect + uncertainty, check guardrails, and ship/hold with clear caveats.

Executive Summary

  • Primary metric: signup conversion uplift of ~4.2pp with 95% CI \([1.1pp, 7.3pp]\).
  • Guardrails: no material degradation detected on retention / revenue signals inside the experiment window.
  • Recommendation: ship with post-launch monitoring to confirm downstream stability.

Experiment Setup

  • Variant: product funnel change (e.g., signup flow).
  • Primary: conversion rate (difference in proportions).
  • Guardrails: retention, ARPU/revenue per user, and any friction proxy (drop-offs, latency).
  • Pre-test: minimum detectable effect + power-based sample sizing.

Methodology

  1. Randomization checks. Confirm balance on key covariates and pre-period behavior.
  2. Primary analysis. Estimate uplift and 95% CI; segment slices for heterogeneity (but avoid p-hacking).
  3. Guardrail analysis. Ensure any downstream degradation is below a “materiality” threshold.
  4. Decision framing. Translate effect sizes into business impact and rollout plan.

Interpretation (how to read the result)

The CI expresses uncertainty: the data supports a positive uplift with high confidence, but the exact magnitude could plausibly be closer to +1pp or as high as +7pp. That range informs rollout scope and monitoring intensity.

Next Steps

  • Rollout: phased rollout with a holdout to validate retention/revenue in production.
  • Sequential testing: adopt sequential monitoring to reduce time-to-decision while preserving rigor.
  • Experiment system: standard templates for readouts, pre-registration, and metric definitions to scale experimentation.
