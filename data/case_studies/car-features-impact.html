Case Study • Data Analysis & Statistics

This case study describes an **exploratory data analysis (EDA) and regression profiling project** designed to isolate the impact of vehicle features on resale prices. By utilizing Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression models, the project quantifies the partial effects of age, mileage, and brand segments.

Executive Summary

  • Business Problem: Vehicle acquisition managers struggled to set consistent buy-in trade prices, leading to subjective valuations, slow sales velocity, and margin variance.
  • Analytical Approach: Performed bivariate profiling (scatter, box, and correlation plots) followed by OLS linear regression to calculate isolated coefficient weights.
  • Statistical Insight: OLS results proved that **each additional year of age reduces a vehicle's value by an average of $1,154** (p < 0.001) while holding mileage constant, and each mile driven reduces value by $0.06.
  • Stakeholder Delivery: Surfaced statistical coefficients inside a Power BI price matrix tool, enabling acquisition reps to estimate base values and justify appraisals to sellers.

Business Problem & Value Optimization

For fleet leasing operators and used car dealerships, purchasing vehicles at the correct trade-in price is the foundation of profitability. If buyers pay a premium for trade-in inventory, they squeeze the margin out of the subsequent resale.

However, valuation is highly subjective:

  • The Multicollinearity Trap: Older cars naturally have higher mileage. Simple averages fail to isolate whether a price drop is caused by vehicle wear (mileage) or style obsolescence (age).
  • Premium Brand Premia: Premium brands command a pricing premium on the lot. Managers need to know exactly how much of that premium remains after 5 years or 80,000 miles.
  • Evidence-Backed Negotiations: When customers trade in their cars, they demand top dollar. Sales staff need hard, statistical evidence to justify their offers (e.g., "The market average value drop for this model year is $3,200 due to its specific mileage category").

Bivariate Profiling & EDA Insights

Before model building, we conducted thorough exploratory data analysis on the listing dataset:

  • Mileage vs. Price: Correlation analysis revealed a strong negative correlation of -0.74. Scatter plots showed a non-linear curvature, indicating depreciation is steepest during the first 30,000 miles.
  • Brand Tier Boxplots: Boxplots confirmed that premium brand pricing exhibits wider variance and a higher median compared to economy tiers, but depreciates at a faster rate.
  • Fuel Type Trends: Hybrids and Electric vehicles show higher initial pricing but faster depreciation slopes compared to traditional gas engines, reflecting market uncertainty around battery degradation.

OLS Regression Analysis

To isolate the effects of highly correlated variables, we trained an Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression model. The coefficients tell us the clean impact of each feature on the listing price while holding all other features constant.

OLS Feature Impact Analysis (Python)

This code snippet demonstrates loading data, training an OLS regression using statsmodels, and outputting the statistical summary: 

import pandas as pd
import statsmodels.api as sm

# 1. Prepare target and independent variables
# We include one-hot encoded categories (dropping one reference category for each)
df_model = pd.get_dummies(
    df[["price", "age", "mileage", "brand_segment", "fuel_type"]],
    drop_first=True
)

# Convert boolean columns to integer (0 or 1) for regression
bool_cols = df_model.select_dtypes(include=["bool"]).columns
df_model[bool_cols] = df_model[bool_cols].astype(int)

# 2. Define dependent variable (y) and independent variables (X)
y = df_model["price"]
X = df_model.drop(columns=["price"])

# 3. Add constant intercept (required for statsmodels OLS)
X = sm.add_constant(X)

# 4. Fit Ordinary Least Squares regression model
ols_model = sm.OLS(y, X).fit()

# 5. Output statistical summary coefficients and p-values
print(ols_model.summary())

# Extract specific coefficient estimates for business review
age_coef = ols_model.params["age"]
mileage_coef = ols_model.params["mileage"]
print(f"\nPrice reduction per year (holding mileage constant): ${abs(age_coef):.2f}")
print(f"Price reduction per mile (holding age constant): ${abs(mileage_coef):.4f}")

Limitations & Future Scope

  • Linearity Assumptions: OLS assumes linear relationships. However, depreciation curves are highly non-linear (e.g., initial 10% value drop when driven off the lot). Using log-transformed target variables or tree-based regression (XGBoost) captures these dynamics more accurately.
  • Omitted Variable Bias: The dataset lack vehicle condition ratings (e.g., dent count, tire tread depth). Incorporating a categorical vehicle condition feature would improve model fit (\(R^2\)) and reduce appraisal errors.
