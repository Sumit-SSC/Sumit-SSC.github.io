Executive sales dashboards are a staple of business analytics and analytics engineering: one source of truth for revenue, margin, and growth that leadership can use for planning and accountability. This case study covers data modeling, DAX design, and storytelling in Power BI, aligned with senior business analyst and analytics engineer roles.

Executive Summary

Goal: Give leadership a single source of truth for sales performance and margin health.

Approach: Fact/dimension modeling; DAX KPIs (YoY, MoM, mix); drill-through for region and segment; publish to Power BI Service.

Insights: Identified margin erosion in two regions and surfaced top underperforming SKUs for targeted action.

Outcome: Dashboards used in monthly business reviews; reduced ad hoc report requests and conflicting numbers.

Business Context

Sales and finance need consistent metrics: revenue, margin, growth rates, and contribution by region, segment, and product. When each team uses different definitions or sources, planning and accountability break down. This project focused on a single semantic layer and clear, documented DAX so that “revenue” and “margin” mean the same thing everywhere.

Data Model & DAX

A star schema was used: fact table(s) at transaction or summary grain, with dimensions for date, region, segment, product, and customer. DAX measures were built for revenue, cost, margin, and then time intelligence (YoY, MoM, same period last year) and mix/contribution analysis. All measures were named and documented so that report authors and consumers could trust and reuse them.

Results & Storytelling

The dashboard highlighted margin erosion in two regions and listed top underperforming SKUs. Leadership used it to assign ownership and track follow-up. Drill-through and filters allowed regional and segment deep-dives without leaving the report.

Future Work

  • Align with a central semantic layer or metric store (dbt, Cube, etc.) for consistency across BI tools.
  • Automated data quality checks and refresh monitoring.
  • Optional: forecasting and scenario views for planning.
