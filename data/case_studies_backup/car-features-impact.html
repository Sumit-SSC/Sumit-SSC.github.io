Understanding how car features drive selling prices is a classic EDA and dashboarding problem for business and data analysts: clean the data, explore relationships, and communicate drivers to stakeholders via Python, Excel, and Power BI. This case study emphasizes reproducibility, visualization, and actionable insights for pricing and strategy teams.

Executive Summary

Goal: Understand which car features drive pricing decisions in the used car market.

Approach: Data cleaning and EDA in Python; feature correlation and univariate/multivariate analysis; dashboard creation in Power BI and Excel; clear visualization of key drivers.

Insights: Mileage, age, brand, and fuel type emerged as top drivers; interactive dashboards allowed segment-level exploration.

Outcome: A reusable EDA pipeline and dashboards that support pricing and strategy discussions.

Business Context

Dealers and marketplaces need to know which levers (mileage, age, brand, fuel type, etc.) most influence resale value. EDA and dashboards answer “what drives price?” without building a full predictive model first, and they provide a shared view for cross-functional teams.

Methodology

Data & EDA

Raw data was cleaned (missing values, outliers, types) and summarized. Correlation analysis and visualizations (scatter, box plots, bar charts) were used to relate price to mileage, age, brand, fuel type, and other features. Python (pandas, matplotlib/seaborn) was used for reproducibility.

Dashboards

Key charts and tables were rebuilt in Power BI and Excel so that business users could filter by segment, region, or time and explore on their own. Clear titles, legends, and tooltips made the outputs self-explanatory.

Results & Insights

Mileage, age, brand, and fuel type were the strongest drivers. The dashboards allowed stakeholders to see how these relationships varied by segment (e.g. luxury vs. mass market) and to use the insights in pricing and acquisition strategy.

Future Work

  • Formal regression or ML model to quantify marginal effects and predict price.
  • Automated refresh and data quality checks as new listings arrive.
  • Optional: integration with external market data for benchmarking.
