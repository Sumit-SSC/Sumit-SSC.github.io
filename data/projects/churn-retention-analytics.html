Project Overview

Churn & Retention Analytics is a full lifecycle system that takes synthetic subscription data from raw usage, billing, and support events to SQL features, ML risk scores, and a Streamlit retention simulator for ROI‑based intervention decisions.

Executive Summary

  • Business problem: subscription businesses need to know who is about to churn, which customers are worth saving, and which levers actually pay back.
  • What was built: a synthetic but realistic customer base (~100k), SQL feature pipeline, logistic regression + random forest models, and a Streamlit simulator that ties interventions to ROI.
  • Why it matters operationally: links churn scores to actual dollar outcomes instead of stopping at a ROC curve.
  • Key insights: 30‑day usage decline, payment issues, and support friction are strong churn drivers; plan tier and timing windows change the economics of intervention.
  • Indicative impact: the best simulated strategy focuses support‑style interventions on high‑value, high‑risk customers, achieving ~2.3× ROI vs blanket discounts.

Business Problem: Churn Risk & Retention ROI

Churn is a critical business problem for SaaS, telecom, fintech, and any recurring revenue model. Leaders ask:

  • Who is about to churn, and when?
  • Which customers are worth saving? (high value, high risk)
  • Which retention levers actually pay back? (discounts, support, features)

Many churn projects stop at a predictive model and never connect back to decisions. This project goes further by linking scores to segments, strategies, and ROI in a simulator that business users can understand.

Architecture, Data & Modeling

1. How the system is structured

The churn lab is designed like a real subscription analytics stack: a pipeline that starts with lifecycle‑shaped data, runs through SQL feature engineering, trains interpretable + stronger models, and ends in a decision tool that business users can touch. 

Raw Data → SQL Feature Engineering → EDA & Lifecycle Analysis → ML Models → Retention Simulator

Repository layout:

  • pipeline/ – orchestration of data generation, SQL, and training.
  • sql/ – staging, feature tables, and data quality checks.
  • src/ – EDA helpers and supporting Python modules.
  • models/ – stored model artifacts and metadata.
  • app/ – Streamlit retention simulator.
  • docs/ – narrative per‑stage documentation and interview guide.

2. Data & Lifecycle Modeling

Synthetic data is generated to match realistic subscription behavior:

  • ~92,610 customers.
  • Usage, billing, and support logs with seasonality and noise.
  • Plan tiers (Free, Basic, Pro, Enterprise) with different churn profiles.

Lifecycle stages (Onboarding, Active, At‑Risk, Churned) are derived from these logs and used as the backbone for analysis and strategy design.

3. SQL Feature Engineering & Data Quality

The sql/ layer turns raw usage, billing, and support tables into analysis‑ready features such as:

  • 30‑day usage trends.
  • Payment issue counts and dunning status.
  • Support ticket volume and recency.
  • Lifecycle stage and tenure.

4. Modeling & Explainability

Two models are trained for risk scoring: an interpretable Logistic Regression baseline and a stronger Random Forest for performance. SHAP is used to communicate global feature importance to stakeholders (usage trends, payment issues, support load, plan tier).

5. Retention Strategy Simulator (Streamlit)

The Streamlit retention simulator is where scores become decisions. Business users can:

  • Filter by segment (plan, region, lifecycle stage).
  • Set risk thresholds and choose interventions (discount, support, feature unlock).
  • Estimate churn reduction per strategy.
  • Compute intervention cost and retained revenue → net ROI.

This is surfaced in the portfolio via the streamlit_url field when deployed, turning the project into a live, explorative tool rather than a static report.

Analysis, Insights & Business Impact

4. Analysis & Key Results

  • Baseline churn: ~7.58% across the synthetic portfolio.
  • Random Forest performance: ROC AUC 0.96, Precision@Top 10% ≈ 51%.
  • Top 10% risk customers: ~51% churn probability vs 7.58% baseline.
  • Best ROI strategy: priority support on high‑value customers yields ~2.3× ROI in the simulator.

5. Executive Insights

  • Usage decline is the strongest signal. Declining 30‑day usage is a more powerful predictor than static attributes alone, supporting lifecycle‑based reactivation campaigns.
  • Plan tier structures risk. Free/Basic tiers are much riskier than Enterprise, which supports tier‑specific strategies and SLAs.
  • Payment and support friction compound churn. Payment issues and heavy support load together create a 4.5× higher‑risk segment in synthetic data.
  • Intervention timing matters. The 31–45 day window provides a meaningful chance to intervene before churn becomes irreversible.

6. Recommendations & Future Work

  • Prioritize high‑value, high‑risk segments for human‑touch interventions; let low‑value, low‑risk churn naturally.
  • Operationalize usage‑trend monitoring as an early‑warning system for at‑risk customers.
  • Integrate with CRM/billing systems to run the simulator on real portfolios.
  • Automate pipeline scheduling via Airflow/DBT/Jobs in a production setting.
