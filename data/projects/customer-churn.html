Project Overview

Customer Churn Analysis & Retention is a full lifecycle project that scores churn risk, explains drivers, and surfaces actionable segments in a Power BI dashboard so marketing and success teams can target retention offers where they matter most.

Executive Summary

  • Business goal: reduce churn by identifying high-risk, high-value customers and designing focused interventions instead of blanket discounts.
  • What was built: SQL-based cohort and feature tables, an XGBoost churn model with SHAP explanations, and a Power BI dashboard that segments customers by risk and value.
  • Why it matters: without structured churn analytics, teams either overspend on retention or lose high-value customers silently.
  • Indicative impact: targeting the top risk decile with appropriate offers improves retention lift vs. baseline while keeping spend focused.

Business Problem: Who Will Churn, and Is It Worth Saving Them?

Subscription and recurring-revenue businesses live or die on retention. Leadership repeatedly asks:

  • Who is at risk of churning in the next cycle?
  • Which customers are worth saving? (high value, high risk vs low value, low risk)
  • Which levers work? discounts, support outreach, feature upgrades, or letting churn happen naturally.

Ad-hoc analyses and one-off notebooks rarely make it into day-to-day decision-making. The project aims to turn churn analytics into a repeatable decision system: clean tables, a robust model, and a dashboard that non-technical teams can navigate.

Solution Design & Modeling

1. Cohorts & Feature Engineering

The data layer is built to answer lifecycle questions, not just a single model run:

  • Cohorts by signup month and lifecycle stage (onboarding, active, at-risk, churned).
  • Tenure in days/months since signup.
  • Usage trends – rolling activity and decline flags.
  • Support volume – count and recency of tickets.
  • Payment health – failed payments, dunning, and outstanding balance flags.

These features feed both the churn model and the downstream segmentation in BI.

2. Churn Modeling

XGBoost is used as the primary model, with class weights and cross-validation to handle imbalance.

  • Train/validation split and k-fold cross-validation to avoid overfitting.
  • Hyperparameter tuning for depth, learning rate, trees, and regularization.
  • Evaluation via ROC AUC, precision@top-k, and calibration checks.

SHAP values explain global importance (support tickets, payment failures, usage decline) and give per-customer breakdowns for stakeholder conversations.

3. Segmentation & Decision Framework

Churn scores are combined with revenue/value to create a simple 2×2 grid:

  • High value – high risk → top priority for human outreach and premium offers.
  • High value – low risk → nurture and upsell, not heavy incentives.
  • Low value – high risk → scalable/automated touchpoints; accept some churn.
  • Low value – low risk → monitor, minimal spend.

This segmentation is implemented directly in SQL and surfaced as a visual in Power BI, so retention planning meetings can start from clear quadrants.

4. Power BI Dashboard

The dashboard turns model outputs into decision surfaces:

  • Churn risk distribution across cohorts and segments.
  • Views by lifecycle stage, plan tier, and region.
  • Drill-through from high-risk segments into individual accounts.

Stakeholders can filter to a target region or segment and immediately see which customers to prioritize.

Insights & Retention Impact

Key Drivers of Churn

  • Support ticket volume & recency – heavy, recent support interactions correlate strongly with churn, especially when tickets are about billing or reliability.
  • Payment failures & dunning status – repeated failed payments are both a signal of financial risk and a source of frustration, making them a top driver in SHAP analyses.
  • Usage decline – customers whose engagement drops sharply over a few weeks form a high-risk cluster, even if they are still paying on time.

Business Outcomes

In the simulated setting, focusing on the top risk decile—especially in high-value segments—delivers improved retention lift versus random targeting while holding intervention spend roughly constant. The model and dashboard combination gives teams a repeatable playbook for each renewal cycle.
